Lesson 1: Greetings and Introductions

Basic Greetings:

  • Hello - Selam (ሰላም)
  • Good morning - Dehna aderk (ደህና አደርክ)
  • Good afternoon - Dehna adersh (ደህና አደርሽ)
  • How are you? - Endet neh? (እንዴት ነህ?)
  • I'm fine - Dehna negn (ደህና ነኝ)

Practice Dialog:

Person 1: Selam! Endet neh? (Hello! How are you?)

Person 2: Selam! Dehna negn. Ante endet neh? (Hello! I'm fine. And you?)

Person 1: Ene dehna negn. (I'm fine too.)

Lesson 2: Common Phrases

Useful Phrases:

  • Thank you - Amesegenallo (አመሰግናለሁ)
  • Please - Ebakkih (እባክህ)
  • Excuse me - Yikirta (ይቅርታ)
  • Yes - Owo (አዎ)
  • No - Aye (አይ)
  • I don't understand - Algebagnem (አልገባኝም)

Cultural Note: Ethiopians often show respect by bowing slightly when greeting elders or people of higher status.

Lesson 3: Numbers 1-10

Numbers:

  • 1 - And (አንድ)
  • 2 - Hulet (ሁለት)
  • 3 - Sost (ሦስት)
  • 4 - Arat (አራት)
  • 5 - Amest (አምስት)
  • 6 - Sedest (ስድስት)
  • 7 - Sebat (ሰባት)
  • 8 - Semont (ስምንት)
  • 9 - Zetegn (ዘጠኝ)
  • 10 - Aser (አስር)

Practice: Try counting objects around you in Amharic.

Lesson 4: Food and Dining

Food Vocabulary:

  • Bread - Dabo (ዳቦ)
  • Water - Woha (ውሃ)
  • Meat - Siga (ስጋ)
  • Vegetables - Atakilt (አታክልት)
  • Injera - Injera (እንጀራ)

Common Phrases:

  • I'm hungry - Rehabe (ረሃብ አለኝ)
  • It's delicious - Timatim naw (ትምትም ነው)
  • Can I have... - ...yichalal (...ይቻላል?)

Lesson 5: Verb Conjugation

Present Tense:

  • To eat - Megab (መጋብ)
  • I eat - Megaballehu (መጋባለሁ)
  • You eat (m) - Megaballeh (መጋባለህ)
  • You eat (f) - Megaballesh (መጋባለሽ)
  • He eats - Megabal (መጋባል)

The Lion and the Mouse (አንበሳው እና አይጥው) - A Classic Ethiopian Folktale

"Even the smallest friend is worthwhile" - Ethiopian Proverb

Level: Beginner Length: Short Theme: Friendship

Amharic:

በአንድ ቀን አንበሳ በጫካ ውስጥ ተኝቶ ነበር። በዚያን ጊዜ ትንሽ አይጥ ያለማሰብ በላዩ ላይ ሮጠች። አንበሳው ከጽናቱ ተነስቶ አይጡን በጣም በኃይል ያዘ።

"ልጥፍህ!" አንበሳው አለው። "እንዴት ደንብህን ሳታውቅ በእኔ ላይ መሮጥ ተፈቀደልህ?"

አይጡ በፍርሃት ተንቀጠቀጠች። "እባክህ ይቅር በል፣ ወንድሜ! በድንገት ስለረሳሁ ነው። በእርግጥ እመለስልሃለሁ!"

English:

One sunny afternoon, a mighty lion was sleeping peacefully in the Ethiopian highlands. Suddenly, a tiny mouse, not paying attention, scurried right over the lion's mighty mane.

The lion awoke with a start and caught the mouse in his powerful paw. "I shall eat you!" roared the lion. "How dare you run over me without permission?"

The mouse trembled with fear. "Please forgive me, brother! I didn't mean to disturb you. If you spare me, I promise I'll repay your kindness one day!"

Vocabulary Builder

አንበሳ (anbessa) - lion
አይጥ (ayt) - mouse
ተኝቶ (teñito) - was sleeping
ሮጠች (rotach) - ran (feminine)
ጫካ (chaka) - forest
እባክህ (ebakih) - please

Cultural Insight

This story is a popular Ethiopian folktale that teaches the value of kindness and that even small creatures can be important. In Ethiopian culture, animals often feature in moral stories called "ሽምጥ" (shimt) that pass wisdom between generations.

The Wise Farmer (የብልህ ገበሬ)

Amharic: በአንዲት መንደር ብልህ ገበሬ ይኖር ነበር። አንድ ቀን አጎሩ መጥቶ "ለምን እርሻህን በዚህ መልኩ እንደምታሳድግ አላስተዋልኩም" አለው።

English: In a certain village lived a wise farmer. One day his neighbor came and said "I don't understand why you cultivate your farm this way."

Vocabulary:

  • ገበሬ - farmer
  • እርሻ - farm
  • ብልህ - wise
  • አሳድግ - cultivate

The Hungry Fox (ረኃብ ያለው ቀበሮ)

Amharic: ቀበሮ ረኃብ በማደር እያለ አንድ ድንጋይ ላይ ተቀምጦ ነበር። ድንጋዩ ላይ የተቀመጠ አንድ ዶሮ አየ።

English: A hungry fox was sitting on a rock when he saw a chicken perched on the same rock.

Vocabulary:

  • ቀበሮ - fox
  • ረኃብ - hunger
  • ድንጋይ - rock
  • ዶሮ - chicken

Vowel Sounds
